Hi,

I'm Alex, an aspiring biologist and programmer. I grew up in Grey County, Ontario, and have recently completed a BSc. in Biology from the University of Victoria.

I am currently surveilling the local bee population at Esquimalt Gorge Park through a contract with the Gorge Waterway Action Society. We aim to illustrate the diversity of bees and their specificity to native flowers in British Columbia. Previously, I have worked at the Owens Lab, where I modelled the long-term effect of chromosomal inversions on populations and performed DNA quality checks on kelp. I also head several projects with the University of Victoria Horticulture Club, including the newsletter and a local invasive species list. I spend my free time learning Italian, reading Tolkien, and staring at nature wondering about its insides.
