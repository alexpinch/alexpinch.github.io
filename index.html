Alex Pinch


I'm an aspiring biologist and programmer. I grew up in Grey County, Ontario, and have recently completed a BSc. in Biology from the University of Victoria.

Previously, I've worked with the Gorge Waterway Action Society to show the diversity of local bees and their specificity to native flowers in Esquimalt Gorge Park, British Columbia. Before that, I worked at the Owens Lab at UVic, where I modeled the long-term effect of chromosomal inversions on populations and performed DNA quality checks on kelp.

I also head several projects with the University of Victoria Horticulture Club, including the newsletter and a local invasive species list. I spend my free time learning Italian, reading Tolkien, and staring at nature wondering about its insides.
